How SignalLedger works
SignalLedger is a rule-based daily scanner — not predictions, not AI, not gut feel. Every morning it scans NSE F&O stocks and Binance crypto futures using a fixed set of quantitative filters. Every signal is recorded at scan time with the exact entry price. Every result is logged at EOD.

The git history is the audit trail. No cherry-picking is possible.

Crypto filters — Binance USDT perpetual futures
Funding Extreme Long LONG
Funding rate is very negative — market is crowded short. When too many people are short and paying funding, a squeeze becomes likely.
funding_rate ≤ -0.05%
Funding Extreme Short SHORT
Funding rate is very positive — market is crowded long. Overleveraged longs paying high funding are vulnerable to liquidation cascades.
funding_rate ≥ +0.10%
OI Surge NEUTRAL
Open interest up significantly in 24h — new money is entering the market. Combined with price direction, signals conviction.
OI change (24h) ≥ 15%
Volume Spike NEUTRAL
24h trading volume is abnormally high vs the 7-day average. Unusual volume often precedes or accompanies significant moves.
volume_24h ≥ 1.5× avg_7d
Momentum + OI Confirm LONG
Price is up AND open interest is rising — trend move with new money behind it. Not just short covering.
price_change ≥ +2% AND OI_change ≥ 8%
Dump + OI Confirm SHORT
Price is down AND open interest is rising — bearish trend with new short positions being added. Not just long liquidation.
price_change ≤ -2% AND OI_change ≥ 8%
Stock filters — NSE F&O universe (~216 stocks) + indices
OI Buildup Bullish LONG
Futures OI is increasing AND price is rising — long positions being added with conviction. Classic bullish buildup.
OI_change ≥ 15% AND price_change ≥ +1%
OI Buildup Bearish SHORT
Futures OI is increasing AND price is falling — short positions being added. Sellers are entering with conviction.
OI_change ≥ 15% AND price_change ≤ -1%
Near 52-Week High LONG
Price is within 2% of its 52-week high — breakout watch. Stocks near yearly highs are often in strong uptrends and may break out to new highs.
((high_52w - price) / high_52w) × 100 ≤ 2%
Near 52-Week Low SHORT
Price is within 2% of its 52-week low — breakdown watch. Stocks near yearly lows may be in downtrends and vulnerable to further selling.
((price - low_52w) / low_52w) × 100 ≤ 2%
Volume Surge NEUTRAL
Today's volume is significantly above the 20-day average. Unusual volume flags institutional activity or event-driven moves.
volume_today ≥ 2× avg_20d
PCR Extreme Bullish LONG
Put-Call Ratio above 1.5 on index option chain — excessive put buying signals fear/oversold conditions. Contrarian long signal.
PCR ≥ 1.5 (indices only)
How bias is determined
Each signal can trigger multiple filters. Bias is determined by counting long vs short signals:
LONG
More long-biased filters triggered than short
SHORT
More short-biased filters triggered than long
NEUTRAL
Equal long/short signals, or purely volume/OI with no direction
How success is measured
EOD result is fetched at market close. Success is purely directional — did the price move in the biased direction?
Long signal success = EOD price > entry price
Short signal success = EOD price < entry price
Neutral signal success = abs(move) > 1%
Entry price = price at morning scan time. EOD price = closing price fetched after market close. No slippage, fees, or position sizing assumed.

Accountability system
1
Signal recorded at scan time
Every signal is saved to a dated JSON file with the exact price at time of scan. This cannot be changed retroactively — git history proves it.
data/stocks/2026-06-22.json → price_at_scan: 391.95
2
Pushed to GitHub immediately
The cron job pushes to GitHub right after every scan. The commit timestamp proves when the signal was recorded — before the market moves.
git commit -m "Morning stock scan 2026-06-22" → timestamped
3
EOD result patched automatically
After market close, the same file is updated with EOD price, move %, and success flag. No manual input — fully automated.
eod_price: 375.30 → move_pct: -4.24% → success: false
4
Raw data is public
Every JSON file is readable by anyone. No aggregation, no summaries — just the raw signal data. Anyone can verify the numbers independently.

Known limitations
⚠️
No stop-loss or target. Success is purely directional — any positive move on a long is a "win" even if it's 0.01%. Real trading requires position sizing and risk management.
⚠️
Entry price is approximate. The scanner runs at 5:30–6:00 AM IST. The actual tradeable price at market open may differ, especially for stocks after overnight news.
⚠️
OI data for stocks is limited. Kite's quote API doesn't give reliable historical OI, so OI buildup filters only apply to live scans — not backfill data.
⚠️
No slippage or fees. Win rate does not account for brokerage, STT, impact cost, or execution slippage. Actual trading returns will be lower.
⚠️
Filters are static. Thresholds are fixed values in config/filters.json — they are not adaptive to market regimes. A 2% threshold in a volatile week is different from a calm week.
SignalLedger is a personal research tool, not financial advice.
